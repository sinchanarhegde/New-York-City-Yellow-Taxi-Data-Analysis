# New-York-City-Yellow-Taxi-Data-Analysis
To ensure the highest level of data integrity for monthly trip records, I spearheaded a comprehensive data engineering initiative focused on cleaning and transforming large-scale Parquet files using Python. By leveraging libraries like Pandas, I managed massive datasets, removing anomalies and standardizing schemas to create a reliable foundation for downstream analytics. This rigorous preprocessing phase was critical in maintaining accuracy across millions of rows of raw transportation data.

Once the data was refined, I shifted into deep exploratory analysis to uncover high-value operational insights. I specifically identified temporal trends by mapping trip density against time-of-day variables. This revealed distinct morning and evening commute surges, allowing me to recommend strategic cab positioning strategies. By aligning vehicle supply with these peak demand windows.

I performed a multi-dimensional analysis of the NYC taxi ecosystem by investigating the correlation between trip distance, duration, and total fare amounts. This study was instrumental in identifying inefficiencies in existing pricing structures. I also segmented this data by geographic location, examining how tipping behaviors varied across different NYC boroughs.
